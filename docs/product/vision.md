# Product Vision

## The One-Line Vision

**The CRM that anyone can use, on any phone, anywhere — starting the day they sign up.**

## The Problem We Are Solving

The global CRM market is dominated by two extremes. On one end: Salesforce, HubSpot, and Bitrix24 — powerful, full-featured platforms built for organizations with dedicated IT departments, long implementation timelines (6–18 months for Bitrix), and large budgets ($50–$300+/user/month). On the other end: lightweight contact apps and spreadsheets that lack critical features like pipeline management, analytics, and team collaboration.

Between these two extremes is a vast underserved market: the 500 million small and micro-businesses worldwide. They need real CRM functionality — pipeline tracking, interaction history, task management — but they are priced out of enterprise platforms and underserved by simple tools. More importantly, they operate primarily from smartphones, not laptops, and they cannot afford the months-long onboarding that enterprise CRMs require.

## Our Thesis

**Mobile-first is not a feature — it is a philosophy.** The traditional CRM industry built desktop-first products and then created mobile "companion" apps as an afterthought. These apps are crippled versions of the desktop experience, missing key features and requiring users to go back to a laptop for real work. We build mobile-first: the phone is the primary computer, and every workflow is designed for it.

**Zero learning curve is a business requirement.** A CRM that requires training is a CRM that gets abandoned. Our target user — a 5-person construction company owner, a freelance consultant, a boutique retail manager — does not have time for onboarding sessions. If they cannot be productive within 15 minutes of signing up, we have failed.

**The CRM should adapt to the user, not the other way around.** Every business has its own process, its own terminology, its own rhythm. A CRM that forces everyone into a generic "Lead → Qualified → Closed" flow will be fought at every step. We give users the tools to model their actual process — flexible forms, customizable pipelines, automation rules, custom fields — without requiring technical expertise.

## Product Philosophy: Simple. Visual. Powerful.

**Simple** means: minimal mandatory fields, mobile-optimized interactions, contextual help, and zero configuration required to get started. The default state works immediately. Power features are there when needed.

**Visual** means: Kanban boards instead of data tables, charts instead of raw numbers, timeline views instead of logs. Information is understood at a glance, not decoded. The UI makes complex business data obvious to anyone, not just analysts.

**Powerful** means: full pipeline management, cross-team visibility, real analytics, automation rules, offline capability, and integrations with the tools businesses already use (calendar, SMS, email). This is not a toy app — it can run a real business.

## What Success Looks Like

At 12 months post-launch:
- A business owner can sign up, import their contacts, and see their pipeline within 15 minutes
- A field sales team can work fully offline for a full day and sync seamlessly on reconnection
- A manager can run their Monday morning team review entirely within the app
- The product replaces spreadsheets, a contact app, a reminder app, and a basic task manager for our target users — consolidating 4 tools into 1
- NPS > 40; monthly churn < 3%; activation rate (users who complete first pipeline action within 3 days) > 60%

## What We Are Not Building

- An enterprise platform (no complex role hierarchies, no SSO, no on-premise deployment — for MVP)
- A project management tool (tasks are CRM-context tasks, not project plans with Gantt charts)
- An email marketing platform (we log emails; we do not send mass campaigns)
- An ERP or accounting system (we track deal values; we do not issue invoices or manage inventory)
- A website builder or e-commerce platform

These distinctions matter because scope creep into adjacent categories is how focused products become bloated platforms that lose the simplicity that made them good.
