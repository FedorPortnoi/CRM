# Competitive Analysis

## Market Overview

The CRM market is large ($65B+ globally, growing ~14% annually) but poorly served at the SMB mobile segment. The market leaders were built in the 2000s and 2010s for desktop, then retrofitted for mobile. No significant mobile-first CRM for SMBs has achieved meaningful market share.

---

## Bitrix24

**Market position:** Dominant in Eastern Europe and ex-CIS; growing globally as a low-cost Salesforce alternative.

**Strengths:**
- Extremely feature-rich (CRM, project management, HR, telephony, website builder, video calls, all in one)
- Free tier available (up to 5 users with basic features)
- Strong in markets where Salesforce is too expensive

**Weaknesses:**
- Complexity is the defining pain point: onboarding typically takes 1–3 months; most small businesses use 20% of the features
- Mobile app is a crippled afterthought — critical features require desktop
- UI is overwhelming for non-technical users; steep learning curve
- Performance is poor on mobile (slow loading, heavy)
- Customer support for lower tiers is minimal
- Interface is dated and cluttered

**How we beat them:**
- We are 10x simpler with no feature bloat
- Our mobile app IS the product, not a companion app
- Users are productive in 15 minutes, not 3 months
- We focus on the 6 features SMBs actually need daily — not 60 features they'll never touch

**Pricing comparison:**
- Bitrix24 Free: 5 users, limited CRM features
- Bitrix24 Basic: $49/month for 5 users ($9.80/user)
- Bitrix24 Standard: $99/month for 50 users ($1.98/user at scale but minimum is high)
- **Our target:** $15–25/user/month, more features per dollar for SMB use cases

## Salesforce

**Market position:** The dominant global CRM for mid-market and enterprise.

**Strengths:**
- Most powerful and customizable CRM on the market
- Massive ecosystem (AppExchange, consultants, integrations)
- Industry standard — deep talent pool of Salesforce-trained admins
- Highly scalable from 10 to 100,000 users

**Weaknesses:**
- Extremely expensive: Salesforce Starter starts at $25/user/month, Professional is $75/user/month, Enterprise is $150/user/month — and that's before required add-ons
- Requires a Salesforce admin (either internal or consultant) — not self-service
- Implementation cost for a 20-person company is typically $10,000–$50,000+
- Mobile app is not a first-class experience
- Overwhelming for organizations under 100 people

**How we beat them:**
- We cost 3–5x less with no implementation cost
- No dedicated admin required
- Productive the same day — no months-long implementation
- This is not a Salesforce competitor for enterprise. It is a Salesforce alternative for companies that don't need Salesforce.

**Pricing comparison:**
- Salesforce Starter: $25/user/month (max 10 users)
- Salesforce Pro: $75/user/month
- **Our target:** $15–25/user/month, fully self-service, no implementation cost

## HubSpot CRM

**Market position:** Dominant inbound marketing + CRM platform for growing B2B companies.

**Strengths:**
- Free CRM tier is genuinely good and widely used
- Strong inbound marketing integration (forms, landing pages, email campaigns)
- Good UI/UX compared to Salesforce and Bitrix
- Strong onboarding and education (HubSpot Academy)
- Large ecosystem of integrations

**Weaknesses:**
- Free tier is a lead-generation funnel — quickly hits limits that require expensive paid tiers
- Paid tiers are expensive (Marketing Hub Starter $20/month; Professional $890/month — a massive jump)
- Desktop-centric — mobile app is limited
- Strongly optimized for inbound marketing-driven sales; less suited to outbound/field sales
- Complex pricing structure with many add-ons confuses buyers
- Over-engineered for simple sales processes

**How we beat them:**
- We are built for mobile, outbound, and field sales — not inbound marketing
- Our pricing is transparent and linear (per user, no tier jumps)
- We do not require marketing infrastructure to get value from the CRM
- Simpler to use for small teams without a dedicated marketing department

**Pricing comparison:**
- HubSpot Free: limited CRM, unlimited users
- HubSpot Sales Hub Starter: $20/user/month (min 2 users)
- HubSpot Sales Hub Pro: $100/user/month (min 5 users = $500/month minimum)
- **Our target:** $15–25/user/month with no minimum seat count

## Pipedrive

**Market position:** Popular sales-focused CRM for SMBs; known for its pipeline-centric design.

**Strengths:**
- Clean, sales-focused pipeline UI
- Good mobile app (better than Salesforce/HubSpot)
- Affordable for small teams

**Weaknesses:**
- Mobile app still secondary to desktop
- Limited customization compared to competitors
- Reporting is basic unless you pay for the advanced tier
- No offline capability

**How we beat them:**
- We are genuinely offline-first — Pipedrive requires internet
- Our mobile experience is purpose-built, not adapted from desktop
- Better for field sales teams specifically

**Pricing:** Essential $14.90/user/month → Advanced $27.90/user/month → Professional $49.90/user/month

## Key Differentiator Summary

We are not trying to beat Salesforce at its own game. We are the product for the millions of small businesses that Salesforce, Bitrix24, and HubSpot cannot effectively serve — not because they don't want to, but because their entire architecture was designed for a different customer.

Our sustainable advantage: **mobile-first design + zero onboarding friction + affordable SMB pricing.** These are structural advantages, not features that can be quickly copied, because they require rebuilding the product from the ground up.
