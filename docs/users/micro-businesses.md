# User Segment: Micro-Businesses

## Profile

**Team size:** 2–10 people  
**Revenue range:** $100K–$5M/year  
**Industries:** Small agencies, local service businesses (cleaning, landscaping, HVAC, plumbing), small retail, boutique professional services, early-stage startups

## The Reality of Their Work

At this scale, the business owner has started hiring. There is now a team — even if it's just 2–3 people — and that team needs to share information. The chaos of a solo entrepreneur (personal Gmail, sticky notes, one brain) becomes dangerous when multiple people are involved. When two reps don't know each other's client history, customers get called twice, promises get forgotten, and leads fall between the cracks between team members.

The core pain is **coordination without chaos**: sharing client information across the team without building a bureaucratic system that takes hours to maintain. They need a shared source of truth that everyone updates effortlessly.

## Primary Needs

1. **Shared contact database** — Everyone on the team can see every client, lead, and partner.
2. **Task assignment** — Assign follow-ups to specific team members and track completion.
3. **Team visibility** — See what the whole team is working on without a status meeting.
4. **Basic reporting** — Know how the business is performing: deals in pipeline, tasks overdue, who is most active.
5. **Simple onboarding for new hires** — When someone new joins, they should be using the CRM the same day.

## What They Don't Need (at MVP)

- Multi-department pipelines (they have one team doing one thing)
- Advanced role-based permissions (everyone trusts everyone at this size)
- Complex analytics (the business owner wants simple numbers, not dashboards)
- API access or custom integrations

## How They Use the CRM

**The business owner** uses the dashboard to see deal pipeline value and task completion rates. They use the team activity report to understand who is doing what without micromanaging.

**Sales reps or account managers** use it daily: check tasks in the morning, update deal stages after calls, add notes to contacts.

**The admin** manages contact imports, keeps tags and pipelines organized, onboards new team members.

## Common Scenarios

- Plumbing company with 3 field technicians and 1 office admin: CRM tracks all service clients, appointment scheduling, follow-up quotes, and technician assignments.
- 5-person marketing agency: CRM tracks prospects in a sales pipeline, manages client onboarding tasks, logs all client calls and emails.
- Small real estate team (1 agent + 2 buyer's agents + 1 admin): Pipeline for buyers in different stages (Looking → Offer → Closing), shared contact database for all leads, calendar integration for property showings.

## Acquisition and Pricing Sensitivity

Micro-businesses are making a team decision on software. The business owner approves it; the team uses it. Key dynamics:
- ROI needs to be visible to the business owner within 30 days
- Per-user pricing means the math is clear: "We're 5 people, that's $120/month — is it worth it?"
- They often have tried and failed with a previous CRM (Bitrix, HubSpot) and were frustrated by complexity
- The pitch that resonates: "Your team will actually use this one" — ease of adoption is the differentiator

## Metrics That Matter

- **Team adoption rate:** % of invited users who are daily active within 2 weeks
- **Contacts shared across users:** Are team members accessing each other's contacts? (Proves cross-team value)
- **Expansion MRR:** Do teams add users as they grow? (Leading indicator of retention)

## Design Implications

- Role-switching context: when a manager taps into "team view," they see all contacts and tasks; when a rep opens the app, they see their own. This requires clear UI for "my view" vs. "team view."
- Task assignment UI: assigning a task to a colleague must be a single-step action (not a multi-screen flow)
- Shared dashboards: team metrics should be visible to everyone, not just the owner, to build team accountability
- Notification design: when a colleague updates a deal you're watching, you should be notified — but not overwhelmed by noise
