# User Segment: Solo Entrepreneurs & Freelancers

## Profile

**Team size:** 1 person  
**Revenue range:** $30K–$500K/year  
**Industries:** Consulting, design, development, coaching, real estate agents (solo), insurance agents, photographers, contractors, accountants

## The Reality of Their Work

A solo entrepreneur is simultaneously the sales rep, account manager, operations team, and CEO. They wear all hats. Their "CRM workflow" today is typically: phone contacts + personal Gmail + a messy spreadsheet + a string of sticky notes and calendar reminders. They spend more time remembering to follow up than actually following up.

The core pain is **falling through the cracks**: a promising lead goes silent because they forgot to follow up. A long-term client drifts away because they haven't checked in for 3 months. A potential contract is lost because someone else moved faster. These failures are not about skill — they are about memory and systems.

## Primary Needs

1. **Follow-up reminders** — Never miss a follow-up again. Remind them to call/email at the right time.
2. **Contact tracking** — Know who everyone is, what they do, and the last time they spoke.
3. **Pipeline visibility** — See at a glance: what deals are active, which are close to closing, what needs attention.
4. **Quick capture** — Add a new contact in 10 seconds without filling out a long form.
5. **Interaction history** — Before any call, read a summary of all previous interactions in 30 seconds.

## What They Don't Need (at MVP)

- Complex team permissions or role management (they work alone)
- Multi-department pipelines or org charts
- Advanced analytics (they manage by feel, not data, at this scale)
- API integrations or webhook customization

## How They Use the CRM

**Morning routine:** Open the app → see dashboard with today's tasks and any overdue items → know exactly what to do first.

**During client conversations:** Look up the contact's history before calling; take a quick note or log a call right after hanging up.

**On the go:** Add a new contact from a networking event by photographing a business card; set a reminder to follow up next week.

**Weekly review:** Glance at pipeline — how many active deals, what stage are they in, are any going stale?

## Acquisition and Pricing Sensitivity

Solo entrepreneurs are price-sensitive. They are often spending their own money, not a company budget. The decision to pay is personal. Key factors:
- Must see value within the first session (first 15 minutes)
- $14/month feels reasonable if it clearly replaces 3 separate tools
- Annual pricing ($168/year) feels more like a "tool I use" than a recurring subscription
- Free trial is critical — they want to try before they commit

## Metrics That Matter

- **Time to first contact added:** Target < 3 minutes from signup
- **Time to first reminder set:** Target < 5 minutes
- **Monthly active rate:** Solo entrepreneurs should open the app at least 5x per week
- **Churn trigger:** If they go more than 2 weeks without opening the app, they will likely cancel

## Design Implications

- Dashboard should be the entire experience for this segment — no need to dig into submenus
- Contact add flow must be the fastest in the industry (name + phone → done in 2 taps)
- Reminder UX must be dead simple: one tap to set a reminder for "tomorrow at 9am"
- The app should feel like a smart personal assistant, not enterprise software
