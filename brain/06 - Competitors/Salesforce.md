# Salesforce

## Overview

The dominant global CRM for mid-market and enterprise. Our target is not to beat Salesforce for their customers — we target SMBs that looked at Salesforce and couldn't afford it or couldn't implement it. Salesforce is our aspirational benchmark, our positioning anchor ("Salesforce power at SMB prices"), and our eventual long-term competition as we grow upmarket.

**Parent company:** Salesforce Inc. (NYSE: CRM, founded 1999)
**Revenue:** $34.9B (FY2024)
**Users:** 150,000+ customers worldwide

## Strengths

- Most powerful and customizable CRM on the market — genuinely enterprise-grade
- Massive ecosystem: AppExchange (7,000+ apps), global consultants, integrations with everything
- Industry standard — deep talent pool of Salesforce-trained administrators and developers
- Highly scalable from 10 to 100,000 users with no architecture changes
- Strong AI features (Einstein AI) for lead scoring, opportunity insights, forecasting
- Virtually every major software tool integrates with Salesforce
- Strong compliance certifications: SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, FedRAMP

## Weaknesses

- **Extremely expensive:** Starter: $25/user/month; Professional: $75/user/month; Enterprise: $150/user/month — before required add-ons that easily double the cost
- **Requires a Salesforce admin:** Not self-service. Most companies under 200 people need to hire a dedicated admin ($70,000–$120,000/year) or pay consulting firms ($150–$300/hour)
- **Implementation cost:** A 20-person company implementation typically costs $10,000–$50,000+ in consulting
- **Mobile app is not a first-class experience:** Built desktop-first; mobile is an afterthought
- **Overwhelming for small organizations:** The platform was designed for 500+ person sales organizations with defined roles and processes
- **6–18 month implementations for complex deployments:** Most SMBs cannot afford this time or cost

## How We Beat Them

We don't compete with Salesforce for enterprise. We compete for SMBs that Salesforce excludes by price and complexity:

| Area | Salesforce | Us |
|------|-----------|-----|
| Entry price | $25/user/month (Starter) | $14/user/month |
| Implementation | $10,000–$50,000+ | $0 |
| Time to productivity | 3–18 months | 15 minutes |
| Admin required | Yes | No |
| Mobile | Afterthought | Primary product |
| Offline | No | Full offline-first |

**The positioning:** "We're what you wish Salesforce was, if Salesforce was made for companies your size."

## Watch Out For

- Salesforce Starter tier is increasingly competitive for small teams
- Their mobile app investments are growing — closing the mobile gap
- Salesforce has essentially unlimited resources to improve any weakness
- HubSpot (which has better UX than Salesforce) is their primary threat; we're a footnote for now
