# Solo Entrepreneurs

## Profile

- **Team size:** 1 person
- **Revenue range:** $30K–$500K/year
- **Industries:** Consulting, design, development, coaching, real estate agents (solo), insurance agents, photographers, accountants

## The Reality

A solo entrepreneur is simultaneously the sales rep, account manager, operations, and CEO. Their "CRM" today: phone contacts + personal Gmail + a messy spreadsheet + sticky notes. They spend more time remembering to follow up than actually following up.

**Core pain:** Falling through the cracks. A promising lead goes silent because they forgot to follow up. A long-term client drifts away because there was no check-in for 3 months.

## Primary Needs

1. **Follow-up reminders** — Never miss a follow-up again (see [[Task Management]])
2. **Contact tracking** — Know who everyone is and the last time they spoke
3. **Pipeline visibility** — See active deals at a glance
4. **Quick capture** — Add a new contact in 10 seconds (see [[Smart Data Entry]])
5. **History before calls** — Read all previous interactions in 30 seconds ([[Interaction History]])

## What They Don't Need (MVP)

- Complex team permissions or role management (they work alone)
- Multi-department pipelines
- Advanced analytics (they manage by feel at this scale)
- API integrations

## Pricing Sensitivity

- Price-sensitive: spending their own money, not a company budget
- $14/month is reasonable if it clearly replaces 3+ tools
- Annual pricing ($168/year) feels more acceptable than a subscription
- Free trial is critical — they must try before committing

## Key Metrics

- **Time to first contact added:** Target < 3 minutes from signup
- **Time to first reminder set:** Target < 5 minutes from signup
- **Weekly active sessions:** Should open app ≥ 5x per week
- **Churn signal:** > 2 weeks without opening → likely to cancel

## Design Implications

- Dashboard should be the complete experience — no need for submenus
- Contact add flow must be the fastest in the industry (name + phone → done in 2 taps)
- One-tap reminder: "Follow up tomorrow at 9am" should be a single button
- App should feel like a smart personal assistant, not enterprise software
