# Micro-Businesses

## Profile

- **Team size:** 2–10 people
- **Revenue range:** $100K–$5M/year
- **Industries:** Small agencies, local service businesses (cleaning, landscaping, HVAC), small retail, boutique professional services, early-stage startups

## The Reality

The business owner has started hiring. There's now a team — even if it's just 2–3 people — and that team needs to share information. When multiple people are involved, the chaos of personal Gmail and sticky notes becomes dangerous. Two reps don't know each other's client history; customers get called twice; leads fall through the cracks between team members.

**Core pain:** Coordination without chaos. Sharing client information across the team without building a bureaucratic system that takes hours to maintain.

## Primary Needs

1. **Shared contact database** — Everyone sees every client, lead, partner ([[Contact Management]])
2. **Task assignment** — Assign follow-ups to specific team members ([[Task Management]])
3. **Team visibility** — See what the whole team is working on
4. **Basic reporting** — Deals in pipeline, tasks overdue, team activity ([[Reporting Dashboard]])
5. **Simple onboarding for new hires** — New person productive the same day

## What They Don't Need (MVP)

- Multi-department pipelines
- Advanced role-based permissions
- API access or custom integrations

## Common Scenarios

- **Plumbing company (3 technicians + 1 admin):** CRM tracks service clients, appointment scheduling, follow-up quotes, technician assignments
- **5-person marketing agency:** Pipeline for prospects, client onboarding tasks, all client calls logged
- **Small real estate team:** Pipeline for buyers in different stages, shared lead database, calendar integration for property showings

## Acquisition Dynamics

- Business owner approves; team uses it
- ROI needs to be visible to owner within 30 days
- Per-user pricing: "5 people × $24 = $120/month — is it worth it?"
- Often tried Bitrix24 or HubSpot before and were frustrated by complexity
- The pitch that resonates: "Your team will actually use this one"

## Key Metrics

- **Team adoption rate:** % of invited users daily active within 2 weeks
- **Contacts shared across users:** Are team members accessing each other's contacts? (Proves cross-team value)
- **Expansion MRR:** Do teams add users as they grow? (Leading indicator of retention)

## Design Implications

- Role-switching context: manager opening app should see team overview; rep should see their personal tasks + pipeline
- Task assignment UI must be a single-step action — not a multi-screen flow
- Shared dashboards visible to all — builds team accountability
- Notification noise control: notify about relevant updates without overwhelming anyone
