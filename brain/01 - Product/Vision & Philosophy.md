# Vision & Philosophy

## One-Line Vision

**The CRM that anyone can use, on any phone, anywhere — starting the day they sign up.**

## The Problem

The CRM market has two broken extremes:

**Too complex:** [[Bitrix24]], [[Salesforce]] — powerful but require IT departments, months of onboarding, and enterprise budgets. Small businesses cannot afford this overhead in money, time, or team capacity.

**Too simple:** Basic contact apps and spreadsheets — missing pipeline management, analytics, automation, and team collaboration.

Between these extremes is a 500M+ business gap: small companies that need real CRM functionality but are priced out of enterprise platforms and underserved by simple tools.

## Our Thesis

**Mobile-first is a philosophy, not a feature.** Traditional CRMs built desktop-first, then shipped mobile apps as afterthoughts. We build mobile-first: the phone is the primary computer, every workflow is touch-designed, and offline operation is guaranteed. See [[Mobile Field Access]] for how this manifests technically.

**Zero learning curve is a business requirement.** A CRM that requires training gets abandoned. Our [[Built-In Learning]] system ensures users are productive within 15 minutes of signup.

**The CRM adapts to you.** [[Custom Workflows]] and flexible data entry (see [[Smart Data Entry]]) mean users don't change their process to fit the CRM — the CRM fits their process.

## Product Philosophy: Simple. Visual. Powerful.

**Simple** — Minimal mandatory fields. Touch-optimized. Contextual help. Default state works immediately.

**Visual** — Kanban boards ([[Kanban Boards]]) instead of data tables. Charts ([[Reporting Dashboard]]) instead of raw numbers. Timelines ([[Interaction History]]) instead of logs. Complex business data made obvious.

**Powerful** — Full pipeline management ([[Sales Pipeline]]), analytics ([[Sales Funnel Analytics]]), offline capability ([[Mobile Field Access]]), automation ([[Custom Workflows]]), and integrations. Not a toy — a real business tool.

## What We Are NOT Building

- An enterprise platform (no complex role hierarchies, no on-premise deployment for MVP)
- A project management tool (tasks exist in CRM context only)
- An email marketing platform (we log emails; we don't send campaigns)
- An ERP or accounting system (we track deal values; we don't invoice)
- A website builder or e-commerce platform

These distinctions matter because scope creep into adjacent categories is how focused products become bloated platforms that lose the simplicity that made them good. See [[Open Questions]] for active debates on scope.

## Success Metrics at 12 Months

- User can sign up → import contacts → see pipeline → **in under 15 minutes**
- Field teams can work **fully offline for a full day** and sync seamlessly
- The app **replaces 4 tools**: contact app + spreadsheet + reminder app + task manager
- NPS > 40; monthly churn < 3%; activation rate > 60%
