# Competitive Landscape

## Market Overview

The global CRM market is $65B+ and growing ~14% annually. Despite this size, it is deeply underserved at the **mobile-first SMB segment**. Every major CRM was built for desktop in the 2000s–2010s.

## The Three Main Competitors

### [[Bitrix24]] — Primary Displacement Target

Bitrix24 is our most direct comparison for the SMB market and our most common displacement scenario. Many of our target customers tried Bitrix and failed to adopt it.

**Why customers leave Bitrix:** Complexity. The platform does everything but does it all in a way that requires an IT administrator, months of configuration, and extensive team training. Most SMBs use 15–20% of its features.

**How we win:**
- 10x simpler UI
- Mobile-first (Bitrix mobile is a crippled afterthought)
- Productive same day vs. 3 months onboarding
- Focused feature set vs. feature bloat

### [[Salesforce]] — Aspirational Benchmark

We don't compete with Salesforce for their customers — we compete for SMBs that looked at Salesforce and couldn't afford it or couldn't implement it.

**Price point:** Salesforce Starter is $25/user/month minimum; Enterprise is $150/user/month. A 10-person team would pay $18,000/year before add-ons.

**How we win:** We cost 3–5x less with zero implementation cost. "Salesforce for SMBs" is our positioning.

### [[HubSpot]] — Closest UX Peer

HubSpot has the best UX in the category after us. Its free CRM tier is genuinely good. But its paid tiers are expensive and it's optimized for inbound marketing-driven sales, not outbound/field sales.

**HubSpot Sales Hub Pro:** $100/user/month minimum 5 users = $500/month floor before you get advanced features. That's $6,000/year.

**How we win:** We're mobile-first and field-optimized. We're 4x cheaper at the mid-tier. We don't require a marketing function to get value from the CRM.

## Our Sustainable Advantage

Three structural advantages that cannot be quickly copied:

1. **Mobile-first architecture** — You can't bolt offline-first onto an existing desktop codebase. Competitors would need to rebuild from scratch.
2. **Zero-onboarding philosophy** — This requires saying NO to features constantly. It's a product culture, not a feature.
3. **SMB-specific pricing** — Our per-seat model with no minimums is impossible for platforms that have sales teams, complex contracts, and enterprise overhead.
